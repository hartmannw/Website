Subword-based Keyword Spotting

For languages with limitied training resources, out-of-vocabulary (OOV) words are a significant problem. This can be especially problematic for keyword spotting (KWS) where many of the target words are infrequently seen. We explore a subword-based approach to detecting OOV keywords. While a word may be OOV, subsequences of characters within the word may be present in the training data. Depending on the type of subwords used, the number of OOV words can be drastically reduced. In our work, we use several types of subwords and search stategies on the data within the IARPA Babel project.
