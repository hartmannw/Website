I am a post-doctoral researcher in the Spoken Language Processing group at LIMSI–CNRS where I work with Lori Lamel and Jean–Luc Gauvain. I obtained my PhD in 2012 from The Ohio State University. My adviser was Eric Fosler-Lussier and I worked in the Speech and Language Technologies Lab.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in artificial intelligence and machine learning. More specifically, my work is in automatic speech recognition. My thesis focused on robust speech recogntion. Currently I am working on speech recognition in languages with limited resources and auditory scene analysis.
